# Customer Discovery Framework

## Purpose

Customer discovery helps BIVA understand whether a real customer segment has a painful problem, how they solve it today, and whether the opportunity deserves further validation.

## Good Discovery Questions

Ask about past behavior:

- When did this problem last happen?
- What triggered it?
- What did you do to solve it?
- How much time or money did it cost?
- Who else was involved?
- What tools or services did you try?
- What made those alternatives insufficient?

## Weak Questions To Avoid

Avoid leading or speculative questions:

- Would you use this?
- Do you like this idea?
- Would you pay for this?
- Is this a good product?
- Should we build this feature?

## Interview Quality Signals

| Signal | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| Specific recent story | Stronger evidence |
| Existing paid workaround | Stronger evidence |
| Budget owner involved | Stronger evidence |
| Vague interest | Weak evidence |
| Compliment without commitment | Weak evidence |
| No current workaround | Potentially weak pain |

## BIVA Usage

Use this framework when designing customer interviews, interpreting interview notes, or deciding whether problem evidence is strong enough to continue.
