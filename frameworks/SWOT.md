# SWOT Framework

## Purpose

SWOT summarizes strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats. It is useful for strategic framing, but it is not a substitute for customer validation.

## Structure

| Category | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Strengths | Internal advantages or capabilities. |
| Weaknesses | Internal limitations or gaps. |
| Opportunities | External openings or trends. |
| Threats | External risks or competitive pressures. |

## Template

```markdown
## SWOT Analysis

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
| --- | --- |
| TBD | TBD |

| Opportunities | Threats |
| --- | --- |
| TBD | TBD |
```

## BIVA Usage

Use SWOT when:

- Summarizing a strategic position.
- Comparing an idea against market context.
- Preparing a business case.
- Communicating to stakeholders who need a familiar format.

## Cautions

- Do not treat SWOT as evidence.
- Do not use vague entries such as "large market" without support.
- Do not ignore substitutes and manual workflows.
- Do not let strengths hide weak demand.

## Example

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
| --- | --- |
| Clear target segment and manual MVP possible. | Limited evidence of willingness to pay. |

| Opportunities | Threats |
| --- | --- |
| Agencies need better lead quality and faster research. | Existing sales intelligence tools may expand into the workflow. |
