# Evidence Ladder

## Purpose

The Evidence Ladder helps BIVA score how strong the available validation evidence is before recommending GO, REVISE, HOLD, or NO-GO.

## Evidence Levels

| Level | Evidence Type | Strength | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | No evidence | None | Founder belief only |
| 1 | Opinion | Very Low | "People will want this" |
| 2 | Desk research | Low | Market reports, trend data, competitor scan |
| 3 | Customer conversation | Medium-Low | Interview where customer describes past behavior |
| 4 | Qualified demand signal | Medium | Waitlist, demo request, signed LOI, active referral |
| 5 | Paid validation | High | Paid pilot, preorder, paid concierge service |
| 6 | Retention or repeat behavior | Very High | Renewal, repeat purchase, active usage, expansion |

## Decision Guidance

| Decision | Minimum Evidence Expectation |
| --- | --- |
| GO | Level 5 or strong Level 4 with a low-cost next step |
| REVISE | Mixed evidence, weak segment fit, or invalidated assumptions |
| HOLD | Evidence below Level 3 and uncertainty remains high |
| NO-GO | Evidence invalidates core desirability, feasibility, or viability assumptions |

## BIVA Rules

- Do not treat compliments as validation.
- Do not treat a large market as demand evidence.
- Do not treat survey interest as equal to paid behavior.
- Ask what customers already do, not what they imagine they might do.
- Prefer observed behavior over stated preference.
