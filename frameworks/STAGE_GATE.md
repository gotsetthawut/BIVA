# Stage-Gate Framework

## Purpose

The Stage-Gate framework helps determine whether an idea should advance, be revised, pause, or stop. It creates disciplined decision points between validation stages.

## Stages

| Stage | Focus | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stage 0 | Raw idea | Idea intake |
| Stage 1 | Problem validation | Customer discovery summary |
| Stage 2 | Market and feasibility | Market research and feasibility analysis |
| Stage 3 | Solution validation | Validation plan and MVP test |
| Stage 4 | Business case | Business case and model assumptions |
| Stage 5 | Decision | GO / REVISE / HOLD / NO-GO log |

## Gate Criteria

### Gate 1: Problem Clarity

- Customer segment is specific.
- Problem is frequent or painful.
- Current alternatives are understood.
- Initial assumptions are documented.

### Gate 2: Market and Feasibility

- Market has reachable customers.
- Competitive landscape is mapped.
- MVP appears technically and operationally feasible.
- Major legal or ethical blockers are identified.

### Gate 3: Validation Readiness

- Riskiest assumptions are ranked.
- Experiments have measurable success criteria.
- Budget and timeline are realistic.
- Kill criteria are clear.

### Gate 4: Business Case

- Business model is plausible.
- Acquisition channel assumptions are explicit.
- Unit economics have early estimates.
- Decision owner accepts the risk profile.

## Decision Options

- **GO**: Advance to next stage.
- **REVISE**: Modify scope, customer, offer, or model.
- **HOLD**: Pause pending resources, timing, or evidence.
- **NO-GO**: Stop active pursuit.

## BIVA Usage

BIVA should use Stage-Gate when a decision requires discipline across multiple documents or when stakeholders need a clear approval path.
