# Expected Outputs

## Strong Output Characteristics

A strong BIVA output should:

- Identify a narrow customer segment.
- Describe the current alternative.
- Separate evidence from assumptions.
- Score evidence using the Evidence Ladder.
- Identify the riskiest assumption.
- Recommend low-cost validation experiments.
- Include AI risk review when applicable.
- Make a clear decision recommendation.
- Explain what evidence would change the decision.

## Weak Output Characteristics

A weak BIVA output:

- Praises the idea without challenge.
- Uses generic market language.
- Treats AI capability as customer value.
- Omits current alternatives.
- Recommends building before validation.
- Makes GO decisions without behavioral evidence.
- Ignores legal, privacy, or trust risks.
